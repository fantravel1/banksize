Physical Scale: Stacking Dollar Bills

A single dollar bill is 0.0043 inches thick. Stack them up:

  • $1 Million: 358 feet tall (Statue of Liberty is 305 feet)
  • $1 Billion: 67.9 miles tall (above the atmosphere)
  • $1 Trillion: 67,866 miles tall (28% of the way to the Moon)

Real-World Comparisons

What $1 Trillion Could Buy

  • 22 years of ending world hunger ($45 billion/year)
  • Every NFL team 34 times over (~$29 billion total)
  • 3.3 million homes at median U.S. price ($300,000)
  • 200 billion Big Macs at $5 each
  • Free college for 14 million students at $70,000 each

What ICBC's $6.3 Trillion Could Buy

  • Apple Inc. twice over ($3T market cap)
  • 140 years of ending world hunger

Bank Profit Per Second

Major banks earn money at rates that seem impossible:

  • JPMorgan Chase: ~$1,583 per second ($50B annual profit)
  • Bank of America: ~$950 per second ($30B annual profit)

Why This Matters

Understanding scale isn't just an intellectual exercise. When we can't comprehend how large these institutions have become, we can't meaningfully evaluate:

  • Whether bailouts represent good value for taxpayers
  • The true concentration of financial power
  • What systemic risks these institutions pose
  • How much influence they have over the economy

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a million, billion, and trillion?

A million is 1,000 thousands. A billion is 1,000 millions. A trillion is 1,000 billions. Each step up is 1,000x larger than the previous.
