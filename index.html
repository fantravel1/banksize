Common Questions About Bank Size

ICBC (Industrial and Commercial Bank of China) has $6.3 trillion in assets, making it the world's largest bank. To put this in perspective, ICBC could end world hunger for over 140 years with its assets, based on UN estimates of $45 billion needed annually.

The top 4 US banks control 44% of all US deposits, a dramatic increase from just 15% in 1984. This consolidation represents one of the most significant shifts in American banking history.

The world's largest banks collectively control over $50 trillion in assets. This wealth could solve global hunger, address climate change, fund universal healthcare globally, and still have trillions remaining. For example, ICBC alone could buy every NFL team 30 times over, or save 5 times Earth's population from starvation.

"Too big to fail" refers to banks so large and interconnected that their failure would devastate the economy. These banks receive an implicit subsidy worth $83 billion annually from taxpayer-backed guarantees, essentially getting free money from the government.

Over 10,000 banks have disappeared through mergers and consolidation since 1990. This massive consolidation has created financial titans larger than most countries' economies.
